Sugar Cookies

Yields: 23 cookies (46 if using both halves of the dough)




Ingredients for the Cookies: Quantities:
Butter 3 sticks (24 tbsp)
All-Purpose Flour 4 1/2 cups (more for shaping and dusting the dough)
Sugar 1 1/2 cups
Vanilla Extract 1 1/2 tsp
Eggs 1 whole, 2 yolks
Baking Powder 1/2 tsp
Cream of Tartar (or Lemon Juice as a substitute) 2 tsp
Salt 1 tsp
Ingredients for the Glaze: Quantities:
Powdered Sugar 2 cups (more in small amounts if the glaze is too thin)
Salt 1/2 tsp
Water as needed
Egg Whites 1/4 cup
Vanilla Extract 1 tsp
Choice of Food Colorings a few drops

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (176°C).

  2. In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter, sugar and cream cheese until smooth,
    making sure to scrap down the bowl as needed.

  3. Add in the vanilla extract, eggs, cream of tartar (or lemon juice) and mix to combine.

  4. Sift in the flour, baking powder, and salt into the mixture and beat until everything has been
    incorporated and a smooth, slightly sticky ball of dough forms.

  5. Cover the dough in plastic wrap and leave it in the fridge for 1 hour to set.

  6. Take the dough out of the fridge and begin rolling it out until it's a 1/4 inch thick on a well floured
    surface. Using your choice of cookie cutters, cut out the dough and place onto a floured sheet pan.
    Feel free to re-roll any leftover dough or left it in the fridge for later.

  7. Bake for 8-10 minutes or until edges are golden brown. Leave to cool completely.

  8. In a separate bowl, sift the powdered sugar and salt. In a corner of the bowl, pour in the
    vanilla extract and egg whites. Start to whisk everything together and incorporate more of
    the powdered sugar until no lumps remain and the glaze is a smooth, white paste.

  9. Decorate as desired. Make sure to chill the cookies in the fridge for the glaze to set if needed.

  10. Enjoy.
